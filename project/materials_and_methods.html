Materials and Methods

1. Materials

All chemicals were used as received without further purification:

  • Cobalt(II) nitrate hexahydrate (Co(NO₃)₂·6H₂O, ≥98%, Sigma-Aldrich)
  • Zinc nitrate hexahydrate (Zn(NO₃)₂·6H₂O, ≥98%, Sigma-Aldrich)
  • Sodium hydroxide (NaOH, ≥98%, Merck)
  • Ethylene glycol (C₂H₆O₂, ≥99%, Merck)
  • Phenylboronic acid (C₆H₇BO₂, ≥95%, Sigma-Aldrich)
  • Bromobenzene (C₆H₅Br, ≥99%, Sigma-Aldrich)
  • Ethanol (C₂H₅OH, ≥99.5%, Merck)
  • Deionized water (18.2 MΩ·cm, prepared in-house)

2. Synthesis of Co₃O₄-ZnO Nanocomposites

In a typical synthesis:

  1. 1.0 mmol Co(NO₃)₂·6H₂O and 1.0 mmol Zn(NO₃)₂·6H₂O were dissolved in 30 mL ethylene glycol under magnetic stirring.
  2. A solution of 8.0 mmol NaOH in 10 mL ethylene glycol was added dropwise to the above solution, resulting in a purple precipitate.
  3. The mixture was transferred to a 50 mL Teflon-lined stainless-steel autoclave and heated at 160°C for 12 hours.
  4. After cooling to room temperature, the precipitate was centrifuged, washed with deionized water and ethanol three times each.
  5. The product was dried at 60°C overnight and calcined at 400°C for 2 hours in air with a heating rate of 2°C/min.

3. Characterization Techniques

  • X-ray diffraction (XRD) patterns were recorded on a Bruker D8 Advance diffractometer using Cu Kα radiation (λ = 1.5418 Å) in the 2θ range of 20-80°.
  • Transmission electron microscopy (TEM) images were obtained using a JEOL JEM-2100 microscope operating at 200 kV.
  • Nitrogen adsorption-desorption isotherms were measured at 77 K using a Micromeritics ASAP 2020 analyzer.
  • X-ray photoelectron spectroscopy (XPS) was performed on a Thermo Scientific ESCALAB 250Xi spectrometer with Al Kα radiation.

4. Catalytic Activity Tests

The Suzuki-Miyaura cross-coupling reaction was carried out in a 25 mL round-bottom flask equipped with a magnetic stirrer. Typically, phenylboronic acid (1.0 mmol), bromobenzene (0.5 mmol), NaOH (2.0 mmol), catalyst (0.5 mol%), ethanol (5 mL), and water (1 mL) were added to the flask. The reaction mixture was stirred at 25°C for 2 hours. The products were analyzed using a Shimadzu GC-2014 gas chromatograph equipped with a flame ionization detector (FID) and a DB-5 capillary column.
