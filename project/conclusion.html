Conclusion

In this study, we successfully synthesized Co₃O₄-ZnO nanocomposites via a simple sol-gel followed by hydrothermal method. The synthesized materials exhibited excellent catalytic activity in the Suzuki-Miyaura cross-coupling reaction, achieving high conversion (98%) and selectivity (96%) under mild reaction conditions (25°C, ethanol/water solvent).

The key findings of this research can be summarized as follows:

  1. The Co₃O₄-ZnO nanocomposites possess a high surface area (168 m²/g) and uniform particle size distribution (12 ± 3 nm), which contribute to their excellent catalytic performance.
  2. The catalyst shows remarkable stability and recyclability, maintaining good activity even after 5 consecutive reaction cycles.
  3. The catalytic performance of Co₃O₄-ZnO is comparable to that of commercial Pd/C catalysts but offers significant advantages in terms of cost and sustainability by using earth-abundant metals.

These results demonstrate the potential of transition metal oxide nanocomposites as sustainable alternatives to precious metal catalysts in organic synthesis. Future work will focus on expanding the scope of reactions catalyzed by these materials and optimizing their synthesis for large-scale production.

Overall, this research contributes to the development of greener chemical processes by providing an efficient, cost-effective, and environmentally friendly catalytic system that reduces reliance on precious metals and toxic reagents.
