Abstract

This study investigates the synthesis and catalytic properties of novel transition metal oxide nanocomposites for sustainable organic synthesis. The research focuses on developing eco-friendly catalytic systems that eliminate the need for toxic solvents and reduce reaction temperatures while maintaining high yields.

Through a combination of sol-gel and hydrothermal synthesis methods, we successfully prepared a series of mixed metal oxide catalysts with controlled particle sizes (5-20 nm) and high surface areas (>150 m²/g). Catalytic activity was evaluated using model coupling reactions, demonstrating up to 98% conversion efficiency with excellent selectivity (>95%) under mild reaction conditions.

Structural characterization via X-ray diffraction (XRD), transmission electron microscopy (TEM), and X-ray photoelectron spectroscopy (XPS) confirmed the formation of well-defined crystalline phases and homogeneous element distribution. The catalysts showed remarkable stability over five consecutive reaction cycles without significant loss of activity.

These findings contribute to the development of sustainable chemical processes by providing efficient, recyclable catalytic systems that minimize environmental impact while maintaining industrial relevance.
