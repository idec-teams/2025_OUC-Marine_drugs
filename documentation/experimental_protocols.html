Experimental Protocols

Protocol 1: Synthesis of Co₃O₄-ZnO Nanocomposites

Safety Precautions: Wear appropriate personal protective equipment (lab coat, gloves, safety glasses). Perform in a fume hood. Sodium hydroxide is corrosive - avoid contact with skin and eyes.

Materials Required:

  • Cobalt(II) nitrate hexahydrate
  • Zinc nitrate hexahydrate
  • Sodium hydroxide
  • Ethylene glycol
  • Deionized water
  • 50 mL Teflon-lined autoclave
  • Magnetic stirrer and stir bar
  • Centrifuge tubes
  • Centrifuge
  • Vacuum oven
  • Muffle furnace

Procedure:

  1. Prepare a 250 mL round-bottom flask with a magnetic stir bar.
  2. Add 1.0 mmol Co(NO₃)₂·6H₂O and 1.0 mmol Zn(NO₃)₂·6H₂O to the flask.
  3. Add 30 mL ethylene glycol and stir until completely dissolved (approximately 10 minutes).
  4. In a separate beaker, dissolve 8.0 mmol NaOH in 10 mL ethylene glycol.
  5. Add the NaOH solution dropwise to the metal nitrate solution while stirring continuously. A purple precipitate will form immediately.
  6. Continue stirring for 30 minutes after complete addition.
  7. Transfer the mixture to a 50 mL Teflon-lined autoclave.
  8. Seal the autoclave and heat at 160°C for 12 hours in an oven.
  9. After the reaction, turn off the oven and allow the autoclave to cool to room temperature naturally.
  10. Decant the supernatant and transfer the precipitate to centrifuge tubes.
  11. Centrifuge at 8000 rpm for 5 minutes.
  12. Discard the supernatant and resuspend the precipitate in deionized water.
  13. Repeat centrifugation and washing with deionized water three times.
  14. Wash once more with ethanol.
  15. Transfer the precipitate to a Petri dish and dry in a vacuum oven at 60°C overnight.
  16. Grind the dried powder into a fine powder using a mortar and pestle.
  17. Calcinate the powder in a muffle furnace at 400°C for 2 hours with a heating rate of 2°C/min.
  18. Allow to cool to room temperature and store in a desiccator.

Protocol 2: Suzuki-Miyaura Cross-Coupling Reaction

Safety Precautions: Perform in a fume hood. Bromobenzene is toxic and carcinogenic - avoid inhalation and contact with skin.

Procedure:

  1. Add phenylboronic acid (1.0 mmol), bromobenzene (0.5 mmol), and NaOH (2.0 mmol) to a 25 mL round-bottom flask.
  2. Add the Co₃O₄-ZnO catalyst (0.5 mol% relative to bromobenzene).
  3. Add 5 mL ethanol and 1 mL deionized water.
  4. Equip the flask with a magnetic stir bar and stirrer.
  5. Stir the reaction mixture at 25°C for 2 hours.
  6. After completion, add 10 mL ethyl acetate to the reaction mixture.
  7. Filter to remove the catalyst.
  8. Analyze the filtrate using gas chromatography.
