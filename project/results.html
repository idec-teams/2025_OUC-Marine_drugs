Results and Discussion

1. Catalyst Synthesis and Characterization

The Co₃O₄-ZnO nanocomposites were successfully synthesized via a modified sol-gel method followed by hydrothermal treatment. X-ray diffraction (XRD) patterns confirmed the formation of crystalline phases corresponding to Co₃O₄ (JCPDS 42-1467) and ZnO (JCPDS 36-1451) with no impurity phases detected.

Transmission electron microscopy (TEM) images revealed spherical nanoparticles with an average diameter of 12 ± 3 nm, showing good dispersion and uniform size distribution. BET surface area measurements indicated a high specific surface area of 168 m²/g, which is significantly higher than that of single-component Co₃O₄ (87 m²/g) or ZnO (65 m²/g) prepared under similar conditions.

3. Catalyst Stability and Recyclability

The recycling study showed that the Co₃O₄-ZnO catalyst could be reused for up to 5 consecutive cycles with only a 7% decrease in activity (from 98% to 91% conversion). XRD analysis after the 5th cycle confirmed that the crystalline structure of the catalyst remained intact, indicating good structural stability.

4. Reaction Mechanism

Based on control experiments and literature reports, a plausible reaction mechanism involves the activation of the aryl halide by the Co₃O₄ component, followed by transmetallation with the phenylboronic acid, and finally reductive elimination to form the coupling product. The ZnO component appears to enhance the adsorption of reactants and stabilize the catalytically active sites.
